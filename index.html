What is Yoga?

Yoga directly translates from sanskrit as "yoke." What this word means is to unite. This union is the joining of one's self with the universe or with nature. Yoga is the grounding of ones self through meditation and often movement. This is done in a myriad of ways and will often look very different in each person's practice. There isn't necessarily a right or wrong way to do yoga but what we aim to do as you go through this site is to inform and expand your knowledge of the practice so that you can connect with yourself and with the world around you. We will begin with breathing and its importance to movement and connection.

Breathing

Breathing is an unconscious function that is part of the autonomic nervous system, similar to heart rate, perspiration, digestion, and much more. However, we have the unique ability to also breath consciously, which in turn can calm us, warm us, and ultimately align us. This breathing is known in sanskrit as Pranayama. More specifically, we use breath through our yoga practice to consciously move from posture to posture. When we can flow from breath to movement we are being intentional. This intention is what guides us through our life. As odd as it may sound, breathing is one of the hardest things to master in yoga. However, even being a novice at it has its rewards. You feel much more accomplished, grounded, and stable not just in your practice but in life.

Chakras

Chakras, or disks in sanskrit are believed to be energy points throughout our body. There are over 100 but we will focus on the seven major chakras in ascending order root, sacral, solar plexus, heart, throat, third eye, and crown. The purpose of yoga when it comes to chakras is to create balance with each one. As the site grows so will the information.

Root

Found in the perinium and denoted by a red wheel. This is the chakra of self-awareness and physical identity.

Sacral

Found in the genitals and denoted by an orange wheel. Connects us to joy, life, and the remover of inhibitions.

Solar Plexus

Found in the naval or belly button and denoted by the color yellow. Connects us to our ego, self-worth, and how we think we are perceived by others.

Heart

Found in the heart and denoted by the color green. This chakra represents our ability to love and be loved unconditionally.

Throat

Found in the throat and denoted by a light blue color. Represents speech and self-expression and how to communicate with others.

Third Eye

Found in the space between our brows or third eye and denoted by the color indigo or deep blue. This is our intuition or ability to see and recognize our path in life.

Crown

Found at the peak of our head and above. It is denoted by a white light or violet. This represents our spiritual awareness.
